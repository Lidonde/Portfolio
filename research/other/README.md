## Episode Summary
Are you actually growing your product, or just stacking signups that never turn into usage? Many SaaS teams get stuck here. More registrations feel good, but they don’t reflect real usage, paid adoption, or a pipeline you can trust. With AI increasingly part of the mix, creating more activity is easy — but it often adds noise instead of signal.

In this episode, hosts **Stijn Hendrikse** and **Brian Grav** discuss practical growth strategies with Alex Laventer, who has led growth at **DataStax** and currently drives go-to-market for an AI agent product at **IBM**.  

The conversation dives into:

- What “growth” really means for modern SaaS teams  
- How to split (or combine) growth marketing and product growth  
- Setting up tracking systems you can trust  
- Where AI helps versus where it distracts, including lead scoring and workflow automation  

### Key Points
- **Why signups mislead growth conversations:** raw signup numbers often hide whether users engage or convert.  
- **Where teams lose signal:** without proper tracking, marketing cannot connect campaigns to meaningful outcomes.  
- **PQLs (Product Qualified Leads):** connect product usage and marketing to identify real growth opportunities.  
- **Sales assist with PLG:** strategic collaboration between product and sales ensures growth signals are actionable.  
- **AI-assisted workflows:** can enhance lead scoring, automate routine tasks, but risks over-focusing on vanity metrics.  

### Actionable Takeaways
1. Stop using signups as a sole growth metric; track **usage, retention, and paid adoption**.  
2. Implement **reliable tracking** and dashboards to distinguish signal from noise.  
3. Align marketing and product teams around **PQLs** to drive meaningful adoption.  
4. Leverage AI selectively: automate workflows and score leads, but don’t let it drive false growth signals.  
5. Review and iterate: treat “growth” as an operating system, not just a label.
