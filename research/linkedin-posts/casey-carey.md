## Post 1: AI Visibility vs Real Demand Generation

Date: April 2026 (approx. 1 day ago)  
Link: (insert original post link if available)

### Content
I left last week's #Spryng event with a nagging thought. 🤔 

B2B SaaS marketers have never really cared about impressions.  
Be honest — how many of you actually have “impressions” on your dashboard?  

(I do. But that's another post.)  

And yet… here we are.  

Every CMO is getting hit with the same question right now from their CEO and board:  
👉 “What’s our GEO/AEO strategy?”  
👉 And the uncomfortable answer is, “AI Visibility.”  

Because AI search and answer engines have been gutting organic search traffic, down 45–65% in many cases. So we feel the need to respond. Fear is a powerful motivator.  

Now we’ve replaced clicks with something new: getting cited.  

And if you squint a little, a citation is basically just…  
an impression.  

So, back to my opening thought,  
👉 "Why do we now care about impressions?"  
👉 "Do we have any idea of the value, or are we just chasing volume?"  

Right now, there’s a wave of vendors selling “AI visibility” as if it were a new gold rush.  
- Dashboards. Scores. Rankings.  
- All priced in the tens of thousands.  
- And of course, the "experts" are coming out of the woodwork and are more than happy to sell you consulting.  

But here is the hard truth, if your entire growth engine is:  
- Capturing demand  
- Converting it on your website  
- Optimizing forms and funnels  
- Outbound sequences and nurture streams  

You’re feeling this shift. Hard.  

But if you are thinking more strategically. If you are working to:  
- Build preference before the buyer shows up  
- Shape how your market thinks about your brand and products  
- Show up and engage prospects across channels and stages  

Then nothing fundamental has changed.  
This is just another surface area.  

The game now is:  
- A relentless focus on getting on the Day 1 shortlist  
- Influencing buying criteria before it’s written down  
- Creating a low-risk path to a better future for your prospects  

Not chasing citations.  
Because citations don’t create demand. Great marketing does.  

Curious how others are thinking about this — are you treating AI citations like another vanity metric to make your stakeholders feel good?  

### Key Insight
New metrics (like AI citations) often distract marketers, but true growth still comes from building demand and shaping buyer perception.

### Why it matters for B2B SaaS
SaaS marketers risk chasing vanity metrics tied to new technologies instead of focusing on what actually drives pipeline—brand preference, trust, and early-stage influence.

### How to apply this
- Avoid chasing **new metrics without clear business impact**  
- Focus on **demand creation before conversion**  
- Build content that **shapes buyer thinking early**  
- Use LinkedIn to influence **shortlists and category perception**  

### My Perspective
This is a powerful reminder that while channels evolve, the fundamentals of great marketing—trust, positioning, and demand creation—don’t change.

## Post 2: The New Reality of SaaS Growth (“SaaS-pocalypse”)

Date: March 2026 (approx. 5–6 days ago)  
Link: (insert original post link if available)

### Content
Well, if B2B marketing was getting a little boring, it surely isn't now. Welcome to the “SaaS-pocolypse.” 🙃 

Yesterday at #Spryng in Austin, Peep Laja (CEO of Wynter) laid out what it actually takes to win in today's market.  

The bar is going up. Fast.  

A few takeaways that stuck with me:  

→ Deep integrations aren’t optional anymore – Point solutions are dead. The winners will plug directly into the broader ecosystem — both third-party tools and the customer’s core infrastructure — and deliver value across the business, not just within a narrow use case.  

→ Data is the real moat – AI isn’t the differentiator. Everyone will have AI.  
What matters is the proprietary data that fuels it, and how effectively you turn it into better decisions, automation, and outcomes.  

→ Vertical expertise is becoming table stakes – Buyers are operating in constant uncertainty. That means less appetite for risk. If you don’t understand their industry deeply and can’t clearly prove ROI, you’re already behind.  

→ Relationships still win deals – At the end of the day, this hasn’t changed: people buy emotionally, then justify with logic. They want to trust you. They want to feel like they’re making a smart bet. They want to be associated with winners. When everything else is equal, customer advocacy and relationships decide the deal.  

The takeaway?  

This next phase of SaaS isn’t about more features and AI-washing. It’s about deeper value, stronger trust, and real differentiation where it actually matters.  

Looking forward to Day 2!  

### Key Insight
Winning in modern SaaS requires deeper integration, stronger data advantages, and trust—not just more features or AI capabilities.

### Why it matters for B2B SaaS
The competitive landscape is shifting from feature-based competition to **value-based differentiation**, where trust, relationships, and real outcomes determine success.

### How to apply this
- Highlight **real outcomes and ROI** in your content  
- Emphasize **customer success stories and relationships**  
- Focus messaging on **deep value, not surface-level features**  
- Position your product within a **broader ecosystem**, not as a standalone tool  

### My Perspective
This reinforces that differentiation in SaaS is no longer about what you build—but how well you connect value, trust, and real customer outcomes.
