## Post 1: AI Effectiveness vs Efficiency in B2B Growth

Date: March 2026 (approx. 1–2 days ago)  
Link: [https://www.linkedin.com/posts/sangramvajre_just-wrapped-a-roundtable-with-ceos-scaling-activity-7444360284045066241-Mu1F](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/sangramvajre_just-wrapped-a-roundtable-with-ceos-scaling-activity-7444360284045066241-Mu1F)  

### Content

just wrapped a roundtable with CEOs scaling from $5M to $20M.

not one of them wanted to talk about AI efficiency.

every single one wanted to talk about AI effectiveness.

→ efficiency = doing things right  
→ effectiveness = doing the right things  

AI for efficiency gets you:  
• fewer people doing the same work  
• faster reports no one reads  
• automated tasks that shouldn't exist  

AI for effectiveness gets you:  
• new revenue streams you couldn't pursue before  
• insights that change your strategy  
• capacity to serve customers in ways competitors can't  

efficiency is a one-time win. you cut once, you're done.

effectiveness compounds.

one CEO said it best: "i don't need AI to help me do more of what's not working. i need it to help me figure out what i should be doing instead."

that's the shift.

from "how do we do this cheaper?" to "what can we do now that we couldn't before?"

most companies are still stuck on the first question.

the ones growing fastest have moved to the second.

what's your take on AI efficiency vs. AI effectiveness?

love,  
sangram  

p.s. if you're a CEO trying to figure out how to use AI for growth (not just cost cutting), DM me. happy to share what's working or if you want to join the next CEO roundtable.

### Key Insight

The real value of AI in B2B is not cost reduction, but unlocking new revenue opportunities and strategic advantages.

### Why it matters for B2B SaaS

Many SaaS companies focus on efficiency (automation, cost-cutting), but this highlights that **growth comes from effectiveness**using AI to rethink strategy, not just optimize execution.

### How to apply this

- Shift messaging from **cost savings → revenue growth**  
- Use LinkedIn content to explore **new opportunities**, not just optimizations  
- Share insights on **what to stop doing**, not just what to improve  
- Position your product as enabling **new capabilities**, not just efficiency

### My Perspective

This reframes AI from a productivity tool into a growth lever—SaaS companies that adopt this mindset will differentiate faster.

### Key Insight

Sustainable B2B growth comes from strategic clarity and execution, not increased spend or complexity.

### Why it matters for B2B SaaS

Many SaaS companies equate growth with more tools, more hires, and more budget. This highlights that **efficient go-to-market strategies can drive revenue without added bloat**.

### How to apply this

- Focus content on **simple, high-impact ideas**  
- Share insights backed by **real data and conversations**  
- Highlight **low-cost, high-leverage strategies**  
- Use LinkedIn posts as **lead magnets** (e.g., “comment for slides”)

### My Perspective

This shows that strong LinkedIn content is not just about insights—it’s also about smart distribution tactics that drive engagement and inbound leads.
