## Post 1: LinkedIn is the #1 Channel for B2B

Date: March 2026 (approx. 2 weeks ago)  
Link: https://lnkd.in/erc8w2vM  

### Content
LinkedIn is the number one channel for B2B marketers (for content, social, ads, you know we love it here) so I had LinkedIn VP Marketing Davang Shah on the podcast this week to go right to the source about what's working, what's not, and trends across the platform. 

We covered: 

- What actually works with LinkedIn ads right now  
- Brand vs performance marketing strategies and how they differ  
- How to think about measurement beyond last-click attribution on ads  
- Why 95% of your buyers aren't in market at any given time and what to do about it  

Davang also shares the data behind thought leader ads, what most marketers get wrong about the buyer journey, and how he's leading his own team through the AI shift at LinkedIn.

Listen here:  
https://lnkd.in/erc8w2vM  

PS. Did you know the average B2B deal takes 211 days and involves 22 people?

### Key Insight
B2B marketing success on LinkedIn comes from combining brand-building with long-term demand generation rather than relying on short-term, last-click attribution.

### Why it matters for B2B SaaS
Most SaaS companies focus too heavily on immediate conversions, but this highlights that the majority of buyers are not ready to purchase. LinkedIn content should nurture future demand while building trust over time.

### How to apply this
- Create content for **future buyers**, not just active leads  
- Balance **brand + performance marketing** in your strategy  
- Use LinkedIn to educate consistently rather than sell مباشرة  
- Track success beyond last-click (engagement, pipeline influence)

## Post 2: Marketing Leadership Retreat

Date: March 2026 (approx. 1 week ago)  
Link: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/davegerhardt_we-invited-100-b2b-marketing-execs-to-arizona-activity-7440377883983933441-pR2R  

### Content
We invited 100 B2B marketing execs to Arizona for the first annual Exit Five Marketing Leadership retreat. 

Kicked off last night with a little welcome mixer and dinner here on the lawn and spent the night hearing the same thing: "this is awesome, I'm so happy to be here." 

And we haven't even gotten into the programming yet!

People are here because they want a peer group, a network, and they want to separate the hype you see on LinkedIn from reality; what's going on inside of a $300M revenue cybersecurity company from Texas and a $100M manufacturing company based out of Seattle is much different than what an AI creator with a Substack is telling you.

A peer network is more important now than ever. We hear daily from our members that there's a “ton of change” in marketing leadership now, and you're all under more pressure now because of AI - it hasn't made things easier, just ratcheted up the pressure.

We're building the best peer network for B2B marketing execs with Exit Five, and it's so obvious whenever I get outside of the building and go meet everyone like this.

Today's agenda:

- First going to get a quick workout in before breakfast  
- Keynote from the great Dave Kellogg on what it takes to be the CMO everyone wants to work with  
- Breakout groups based on industry/size/stage to discuss AI (off the record)  
- Workshops on presenting to the board, managing up, team structure/org design, and personal branding  
- Then we're outside all afternoon splitting up into pool, golf, yoga, workout, and back for dinner tonight  

### Key Insight
High-value B2B content is rooted in real-world experiences, peer learning, and community—not just online thought leadership.

### Why it matters for B2B SaaS
This shows that trust and authority in SaaS are built through **relationships and shared experiences**, not just content output. LinkedIn should reflect real insights from actual operators, not surface-level trends.

### How to apply this
- Share **real experiences**, not just opinions  
- Build or participate in **communities and networks**  
- Use LinkedIn to document real-world insights (events, conversations, lessons)  
- Position content as **“insider knowledge”** rather than generic advice

## Pattern Observed

- Strong focus on **community + relationships** in B2B growth  
- Content is **experience-driven**, not theoretical  
- Consistently ties marketing back to **revenue and pipeline**  
- Uses a **conversational, non-corporate tone**  
- Blends **personal brand + company brand (Exit Five)** seamlessly  

---

## Takeaway for Playbook

Effective LinkedIn content for B2B SaaS should combine **personal storytelling, real-world insights, and strategic thinking** to build trust and influence long before buyers are ready to convert. Content should not just inform—it should create connection, credibility, and long-term demand.
