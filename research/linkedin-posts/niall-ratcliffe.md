## Post 2: The New LinkedIn Growth System (2026)

Date: March 2026 (approx. 4–5 days ago)  
Link: (insert original post link if available)

### Content
LinkedIn's rules have changed.
 
I've spent the past 4 years working with the top B2B brands in Europe on their LinkedIn presence.
 
Over the last 18 months, things have changed dramatically.
 
The Old Way:
 
• Take a good profile picture  
• Write a simple headline  
• Talk about your service  
• Share your story  
 
✅ See your DMs flooded with leads  
 
The new way:
 
• Turn your profile into a landing page  
• Think of content like "fishing"  
• Share 2-4 posts per week  
• Track "Intent signals"  
• Outreach to ICP  
• Aim for 800 prospects per month  
 
✅ Start 200-300 conversations per month  
 
The LinkedIn gurus will still tell you that all you need to do is post and you’ll be flooded with leads.
 
But the experience of people with 100,000+ followers is not the experience of companies with under 10,000.
 
Winning on LinkedIn in 2026 is about who can start the most conversations.
 
And you won’t do that from content alone.

### Key Insight
LinkedIn growth has shifted from passive inbound (posting) to active systems combining content, targeting, and outreach.

### Why it matters for B2B SaaS
SaaS companies relying only on organic posting will struggle—success now depends on integrating **content with outbound and intent-driven engagement**.

### How to apply this
- Combine **content + outbound strategy**  
- Track and act on **intent signals (engagement, profile views, comments)**  
- Build a **consistent posting cadence (2–4 times per week)**  
- Focus on starting **high-quality conversations at scale**  

### My Perspective
This highlights a major shift—LinkedIn is no longer just a content platform, but a full-funnel growth engine when used strategically.
